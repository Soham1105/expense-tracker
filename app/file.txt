Based on what the Reports page already has (date/source/direction/tag
  filters, advanced filters, presets, KPI strip, spend pie, bulk Group &
  Classify, quick-tag, groups, shared joy, export), here are suggestions that
   add new value rather than duplicate existing features. I've grouped them
  and noted value/effort.

  A. Faster review & classification (highest leverage — builds on what we 
  just did)

  1. "Needs review" focus mode — a one-click toggle (chip in the filter bar)
  that shows only auto-tagged/unreviewed rows. This directly drains the
  backlog the new needs_review flag creates (the 37 auto-propagated txns),
  and pairs with the existing Confirm auto-tagged button. High value, low
  effort.
  2. Untagged spotlight + completeness meter — a small "82% classified · 41
  untagged (₹38k)" bar with a one-tap filter to the untagged set. Turns
  classification into a finishable task. High, low.
  3. +N more tag overflow chip — rows now show root + leaf (2 chips); a
  subtle +2 chip (hover/tap reveals the rest) so nothing feels hidden after
  the leaf-display change. Medium, low.
  4. Expanded bulk actions — today bulk select only offers Group & Classify.
  Add bulk Mark reviewed, Set flow type, Mark "no tag needed", and Hide for
  the selected rows. High, medium.

  B. Data integrity

  5. Duplicate-import detection — flag likely duplicates (same amount + date
  + counterparty within a few days) with a "possible duplicate" badge and a
  quick dismiss/merge. Protects against re-uploaded statements
  double-counting. High, medium.
  6. Running totals in the table footer — show the summed debit/credit/net of
   the currently filtered rows in the footer (the KPI strip is global; this
  is per-view). Medium, low.

  C. Insight on the page itself

  7. Group the spend pie by leaf — add a "Leaf / Subcategory" option to the
  existing group by selector so you can see petrol (4-wheeler) vs petrol
  (2-Wheeler) right here, leveraging the new separation. Medium, low.
  8. Anomaly highlight — subtly mark a transaction that's much larger than
  that category's typical spend ("3× your usual Groceries"). Medium, medium.
  9. Recurring/subscription surfacing — you already have a /recurring
  endpoint; badge recurring merchants and show "N subscriptions ≈ ₹X/mo".
  Medium, medium.

  D. Power-user speed

  10. Keyboard review — j/k to move between rows, t to open quick-tag, c to
  confirm, e to edit. Makes reviewing dozens of rows fast. Medium, medium.
  11. Quick date chips — This month / Last month / Last 30d / This week next
  to the date range (faster than picking dates). Low, low.

  ---
  My recommended starter set (most value, builds straight on the leaf-tag +
  needs-review work): #1 Needs-review mode, #2 Untagged spotlight, #3 +N more
   chip, #5 Duplicate detection.

   them and I'll fold them in.)
